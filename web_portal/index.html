The transition of the computational graph topology is governed by the structural loss function $\mathcal{L}(\mathcal{F}_t) = \alpha \cdot \text{Tr}(\hat{\Sigma}_t) + \beta \cdot \mathcal{L}_{\text{MDL}}(\mathcal{F}_t)$.

Cooldown Equation

Dynamic Threshold Adaptation

After capacity pruning, the mutation threshold is temporarily raised in proportion to description-length removed to prevent a cascade of unnecessary re-activations: $$\text{threshold}_{\text{dynamic}} = \text{threshold}_{\text{base}} + \gamma \cdot \Delta\text{bits}_{\text{removed}}$$

Here, $\Delta\text{bits}_{\text{removed}}$ is the description-length freed by the pruning pass, and $\gamma \approx 2.85$ is a tunable pacing gain fit empirically.

The Automated Brake

Stabilizing Topological Drift

Inflated dynamic thresholds render the agent temporarily conservative, damping its sensitivity to transient environmental regime noise.

Simultaneously, the active parameter learning rate is damped: $\eta_{\text{adapted}} = \eta_{\text{base}} \frac{\text{threshold}_{\text{base}}}{\text{threshold}_{\text{dynamic}}}$. This feedback loop prevents chaotic structural divergence under severe memory constraints.
